INFO-F420 Computational Geometry Project

Context

This page was created in the context of the “INFO-F420 - Computational Geometry” course at Université libre de Bruxelles (ULB). The goal of the project is to read and understand one or several recent research articles in computational geometry, related to the topic of choice. A creative work must be implemented demonstrating our deep understanding of the concepts described in the articles we read.

Progress

Currently, we have implemented a basic tool that allows to compute the reflections of a light ray in an octagonal mirror integer-environnement. We have started the explanations of the concepts of the article, and we essentially still need to add some important points, illustrate different parts, and mostly add some pertinent images and figures to better illustrate the ideas. However note that our tool does not completely implement the whole scope of the article, but rather allows to illustrate some important parts, such as the finite number of reflections, directions and its general idea. In order to obtain a complete implementation we would have to complement it with a reduction into a triangulated manifold and apply a series of subroutines from computational topology, that would potentially make the whole algorithm faster (actually we are not certain of that point), but the overall outcome of ray-shooting would not change. As this topological implementation is quite time consuming (and we are not even sure we would be able to code it in such short time), and does not bring much, at least visually, to the whole process, we will just focus on explaining its concepts.
